## 1. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Every major English translation of the Qur'an commits the same systematic errors — not random mistakes, but patterned corruptions that:

1. **Turn verbs into nouns** — Qur'anic action-descriptions become ethnic labels (يَهُود → "Jews")
2. **Drop claim-markers** — قَالُوا / "they SAID" is silently removed, turning claims into facts
3. **Flatten Form variations** — Form II (فَعَّلَ = imposing), Form V (تَفَعَّلَ = self-applying), Form VIII (افْتَعَلَ = extracting) are all rendered as Form I
4. **Narrow scope** — broad Qur'anic concepts are squeezed into narrow Western categories
5. **Invert roots** — the actual root meaning is reversed (عَذَاب from عَذْب/sweet → rendered as "punishment" instead of "denial of sweetness")
6. **Import foreign frameworks** — Latin, Greek, and English cultural categories replace Qur'anic ones

**Scale:** 40 core terms documented in the QV_TRANSLATION_REGISTER affect **2,141+ āyāt** — roughly one-third of the Qur'an's 6,236 āyāt are touched by these 40 mistranslations alone. The actual number of mistranslated terms is far higher.

## 2. FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: The Qur'an Defines Its Own Terms
The Qur'an is not dependent on external dictionaries. Allah defines every key term through:
- **Internal pairing**: إِيمَان is defined by الْأَمْن in Q6:82 (same root, same āyah)
- **Contextual demonstration**: صَوْم means abstention — proven by Maryam's صَوْم from speech (Q19:26), not food
- **Root consistency**: every usage of a root carries the root meaning — exceptions require proof, not the rule
- **Structural contrast**: غَيْب/شَهَادَة, حَقّ/بَاطِل, نُور/ظُلُمَات — the pairs define each other

### Principle 2: Roots First, Convention Never
Every word is translated from its root (ج-ذ-ر / jadhr), not from what translators traditionally write. If the root says ك-ف-ر = to cover, the translation says COVER — regardless of 1,400 years of convention saying "disbelief."

### Principle 3: Verb Forms Carry Meaning
Allah's Arabic has 10+ verb forms (أَوْزَان). Each form modifies the root meaning precisely:
- **Form I** (فَعَلَ) — base action
- **Form II** (فَعَّلَ) — intensive/causative (يُنَصِّرَانِهِ = they IMPOSE the naṣārā identity)
- **Form III** (فَاعَلَ) — interactive/mutual
- **Form IV** (أَفْعَلَ) — causative (أَسْلَمَ = to cause oneself to enter سَلَام)
- **Form V** (تَفَعَّلَ) — self-reflective (تَزَكَّىٰ = to purify ONESELF)
- **Form VI** (تَفَاعَلَ) — mutual/reciprocal
- **Form VII** (انْفَعَلَ) — passive/self-occurring
- **Form VIII** (افْتَعَلَ) — self-extraction (اسْتَمْسَكَ = to grasp FIRMLY for oneself)
- **Form X** (اسْتَفْعَلَ) — seeking/requesting (اسْتَغْفَرَ = to seek covering/protection)

Current translations flatten ALL forms to Form I. The USLaP translation preserves form-meaning.

### Principle 4: قَالُوا Is Never Silent
When Allah marks a group's identity with قَالُوا / "they SAID," the translation MUST preserve this marker. It means: "those who CLAIMED [identity]" — not "those who ARE [identity]." The claim-marker is Allah's own qualifier. Dropping it is تَحْرِيف / taḥrīf / distortion.

### Principle 5: No Term Is Translated by a Single English Word
English words carry cultural baggage. Each Qur'anic term is rendered as:
- **Primary translation** — the closest root-based English rendering
- **Root gloss** — the root letters and their core meaning (in parentheses on first occurrence)
- **Expansion note** — a brief note (footnote or margin) explaining the full root field

Example: "...those who COVERED (كَفَرُوا, from ك-ف-ر: to cover, conceal, bury — the same root as the farmer who covers seed with soil)..."

### Principle 6: Names of Allah Are Not Translated — They Are Explained
الرَّحْمَٰنِ is not "the Most Gracious" — it is al-Raḥmān (the Intensely Nurturing, from ر-ح-م / raḥim / womb). The Name is kept in Arabic with a root-based explanation on first occurrence.

### Principle 7: Qur'anic Names Replace Colonial Names
مِصْر is Miṣr, not "Egypt." الْأَرْض الْمُقَدَّسَة is the Holy Land, not "Palestine." فِرْعَوْن is Firʿawn, not "Pharaoh" (unless the root meaning is shown). All non-Qur'anic names appear in "quotes" with the Qur'anic name primary.

## 3. TRANSLATION FORMAT — THREE-LAYER RENDERING

Each āyah is presented in three layers:

### Layer 1: ARABIC (original)
The preserved text. No modification. Full tashkīl (vowel marks).

### Layer 2: ROOT TRANSLATION (the USLaP contribution)
English rendering derived from roots, preserving:
- Root meaning (not convention)
- Verb form meaning
- Claim-markers (قَالُوا)
- Structural pairs
- Active vs. passive voice

### Layer 3: ROOT MAP (reference)
A word-by-word breakdown showing:
- Each Arabic word
- Its root
- Its form (if verb)
- Its root-meaning
- Cross-reference to QV_TRANSLATION_REGISTER where applicable

## 7. WHAT THIS IS NOT

This is NOT:
- A tafsīr (commentary) — it does not interpret. It translates from roots.
- An opinion — every rendering is falsifiable (check the root yourself).
- A new reading — the Arabic text is untouched (Q15:9). Only the English rendering changes.
- A rejection of previous scholars — it is a correction of TRANSLATORS, not of the Qur'an or its sciences.

This IS:
- A return to roots — literally. Every word traced to its root.
- The Qur'an explaining itself — Principle 1 governs everything.
- A lattice product — built on the same root-tracing methodology as the USLaP linguistic database.
- Verifiable by anyone with a Qur'an and a root dictionary.
