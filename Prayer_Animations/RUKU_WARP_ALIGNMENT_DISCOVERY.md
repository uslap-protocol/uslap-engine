## THE CORRECTED MODEL

### Body tissue architecture — three frameworks (verified, observable):

**1. MUQARNAS — المُقَرْنَصَات**
- Positive space: structural solids (bone, fascia bands)
- Negative space: functional voids (joint capsules, disc spaces, fold zones)
- The negative space is WHERE MOVEMENT HAPPENS — not empty, not decorative
- Load distributes through BOTH positive and negative space
- Q30:30: فِطْرَتَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي فَطَرَ النَّاسَ عَلَيْهَا

**2. AL-QINNAB — القِنَّب (stalk + leaves)**
- Stalk: each vertebral body, each long bone shaft
- Leaves: ribs (from thoracic vertebrae), transverse processes (from all vertebrae)
- The body is NOT a stalk. It is a stalk WITH LEAVES at every vertebral level.
- Thoracic leaves (ribs) are the longest, reaching the sternum
- Cervical and lumbar leaves (transverse processes) are shorter but present

**3. BOW — القَوْس**
- Each rib IS a bow (stores elastic energy during breathing)
- Each vertebral arch IS a bow (wraps around spinal cord)
- The intercostal muscles and spinal ligaments ARE the strings
- Bending follows bow physics: outer surface stretches, inner surface compresses
- The "negative space" at joints is where the bow-string relationship changes angle

### Skin fibre direction follows what is underneath:

| Zone | Structure Underneath | Skin Warp Direction |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Mid-limb shaft | Long bone (stalk) | Along the bone |
| Joint | Hinge (negative space) | Around the limb (allows folding) |
| Thorax | Ribs (leaves/bows) | Along the ribs (circumferential in standing) |
| Cervical | Small vertebral arches | Along the arches |
| Lumbar | Thick vertebral arches | Along the arches |
| Pelvis | Bowl (iliac wings) | Along the bowl contour |
| Skull | Dome | Along the dome contour |

### The key insight:

> The body's warp is NEVER one direction in standing. It is multi-directional by design — like muqarnas, which requires arches in multiple planes to distribute load.
>
> **RUKU is the ONE pose that aligns all warp lines into a single direction — vertical, perpendicular to the floor.**
>
> The torso tips 90° → ribs hang vertically. Limbs stay vertical. Vertebral arches orient into vertical planes. The entire body becomes one unified sheet of parallel vertical threads.

> This is the pose of العَظِيم — The Most Great. Maximum structural unity.
