SHIRT — Side Panel (cut 2, mirror one)

Each piece wraps from المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي (back center) around one side to the front opening. Sleeve is CONTINUOUS with body — no armhole, no set-in sleeve. Shown at 1:5 scale. Medium (Bust 92cm). See Measurements tab to adjust.

GRAIN LINE → runs LEFT-TO-RIGHT (horizontal on pattern = along the ribs). Place pattern so selvedge/warp runs across the body, NOT top-to-bottom.
Derivation (analytical, not from lattice directly): BA058 establishes that all body warps are vertical in رُكُوع. BA065 establishes that ribs are horizontal bows. Ribs vertical in ruku → cloth warp should follow ribs → grain horizontal on standing body. This principle is DERIVED from the lattice data, not stated in it.

Each piece starts at the spine (BACK CENTER = left edge of flat pattern), wraps around one side of the body, and ends at the sternum (FRONT CENTER = right edge of flat pattern). The sleeve extends from the shoulder area — continuous with the body, not separate.

KEY DIFFERENCES you can SEE:
1. Left = back center (spine, gold), right edge = front center (opening, blue) — one piece wraps HALF the body from spine around to sternum (see cross-section above)
2. Standard pattern is ROTATED 90° — on the same bolt of fabric, the two patterns sit perpendicular because their grains are perpendicular
3. NO dart anywhere — the curved right edge IS the bust/waist/hip shaping, built into the wrap
4. NO armhole — sleeve flows from shoulder continuously
5. Standard needs 6-8 pieces + separate sleeve. Ruku needs 1 piece per side

PANTS — Leg Wrap Panel (cut 2, mirror one)

Each piece wraps ONE LEG. Anchors at عَجْب الذَّنَب (coccyx), wraps following the 11 خُطُوط, seam on المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي (down the back of the leg). Based on BA058-BA061.

GRAIN LINE → runs LEFT-TO-RIGHT (horizontal on pattern). In رُكُوع, this is VERTICAL on the body (perpendicular to floor).
Derivation (analytical): BA060 establishes 11 vertebral warps wrapping from spine around the leg. BA058 establishes all warps vertical in ruku. Cloth warp following these lines → grain horizontal on flat pattern. Derived from lattice, not stated directly.
KEY DIFFERENCES from standard pants:
1. ONE piece per leg (not front panel + back panel + waistband = 5 pieces)
2. ONE seam per leg down the BACK (not 4: inseam + outseam + center front + center back)
3. NO waistband compressing the sacral fan — drawstring or shoulder suspension
4. NO crotch curve — the piece wraps from posterior around to posterior, shaped on the living body
5. Width = FULL leg circumference (the piece wraps the entire leg, not half)
6. 11 خُطُوط force line zones visible — cloth aligned to vertebral architecture

Assembly Guide

SHIRT — Assembly (3 seams: 1 back center + 2 sleeve)

PREPARATION: Place fabric with selvedge running LEFT-RIGHT (not top-bottom). This is the opposite of standard pattern layout. The warp/selvedge direction becomes the grain line shown on the pattern.
1
Cut 2 side panels (mirror one). Each panel = body + integrated sleeve. Place pattern on fabric with grain line arrow matching the selvedge direction (horizontal). Cut with 1.5cm seam allowance all around.
2
Sew back center seam (المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي) — join the two panels at their straight left edges (back center). This is the ONE structural seam. Press open. This seam runs from back neckline to back hem.
3
Fold at shoulder line — the garment folds so front hangs over back (or vice versa). The front and back panels are now stacked, with the sleeve extending from each side.
4
Sew sleeve seams (×2) — from wrist, along the sleeve underside, to the underarm point. STOP at the underarm. Do NOT continue down the body side to the hem. Below the underarm, the front and back panels hang FREE — this is the wrap opening. Each sleeve becomes a tube; the body remains open at the front and sides below the underarm.
5
Cut front neckline — the back neckline is already shaped (shallow curve, 2cm deep). For the front, cut a V-neck or round neck to desired depth (8-12cm below shoulder). Cut through one layer only (the front layer after folding).
6
Finish edges — hem the bottom, finish the neckline (fold under, bias binding, or leave raw if fabric doesn't fray), finish the front opening edges, finish wrist openings. No interfacing needed — the wrap creates natural reinforcement where layers overlap.
FRONT CLOSURE: The body below the underarm is an open wrap — front panels and side panels hang free. Options:
— Overlap left over right (or right over left) and tie with a sash
— Add button/frog closures at 2-3 points along the front edge
— Leave as open wrap (layer over inner garment / قَمِيص)
— For partial closure: sew side seams from underarm to waist only, leaving lower front open
— For full-closed: extend side seams to hem AND sew front edges together (becomes a pullover — loses the wrap)

PANTS — Assembly (1 seam per leg)

1
Cut 2 leg wrap panels (mirror one). Each panel = full leg circumference width, tapers from pelvis to ankle. Grain line horizontal (selvedge runs side-to-side, not top-to-bottom).
2
Wrap each panel around one leg — the person stands in a slight forward lean or, ideally, in modified رُكُوع. Start at the back of the leg (المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي), wrap around, return to the back. Pin where the edges meet. This IS the seam line.
3
Sew ONE seam per leg — down the posterior midline (the back of the leg). This is المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي / al-miḥwar al-ẓahrī / the stable conductor. From coccyx/pelvis level to ankle.
4
Join at pelvis — the two leg tubes connect at the top. The pelvis wrap portions of each panel overlap or are sewn together at the back center (continuation of المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي up through the pelvis). At the front, the panels can overlap (wrap closure) or be sewn.
5
Waist closure — NO horizontal waistband. Options:
— Drawstring channel at iliac crest level (below the sacral fan, not across it)
— Shoulder straps / suspenders (transfers weight to shoulders, zero sacral compression)
— Self-anchoring: upper edge rolls inward and grips (like a wrap skirt)
— Tie at the side (like a lungi/sarong)
6
Finish ankle — hem or leave raw. The taper follows the body — no elastic cuff needed.

Measurements — How to Adjust

The patterns shown use Medium (UK 12 / US 8) measurements. To adjust: take YOUR measurements in رُكُوع position (or leaning forward 90° at the hip). The circumference measurements will differ from standing measurements because tissue redistributes.

Standing measurements are what standard patterns use — they produce garments that fight the body in every other position. Ruku measurements capture the body at maximum structural unity.

If measuring alone: lean forward on a table at 90° hip angle, arms hanging.

Scaling the Pattern

To adjust: change the WIDTH at each key height (bust, waist, hip, thigh, knee, ankle) to match your half-circumference. The HEIGHT (back length, sleeve length, coccyx-to-ankle) adjusts by lengthening or shortening the vertical dimension. The SHAPE (curves between key points) stays the same — the body topology is consistent across sizes.
