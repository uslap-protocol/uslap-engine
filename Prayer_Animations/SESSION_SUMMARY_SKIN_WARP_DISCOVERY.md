## ROUND 5 — The ribs change everything

**What I did:** Recognised the thorax is NOT a column. It's 24 bows (ribs) wrapping circumferentially from spine to sternum. Using al-Qinnab correctly: spine = stalk, ribs = leaves. Skin over thorax follows the LEAVES (horizontal), not the stalk.

**Key insight:** The body has permanently multi-directional warp. No pose can make thoracic warp vertical because ribs always wrap circumferentially.

**Correction from bbi:** "The neck is not one piece bone."

## ROUND 6 — The spine is NEVER a column

**What I did:** Recognised every vertebra is a horizontal bow (vertebral arch) with horizontal leaves (transverse processes). Even the lumbar "stalk" has horizontal structure. The ONLY pure vertical zones are mid-shafts of the four long limb bones.

**Correction from bbi:** Pushed toward the answer — "which pose would the torso become horizontal so its warp lines become vertical?"

---

## ROUND 7 — The answer: رُكُوع / RUKU

**The logic (4 steps):**
1. Ribs are horizontal bows in standing
2. Horizontal things become vertical when tipped 90°
3. Which prayer pose tips the torso 90° while limbs stay vertical?
4. **Ruku.**

In رُكُوع / rukūʿ: spine horizontal → ribs hang vertically from it → limbs stay vertical → ALL warp lines run perpendicular to the floor. One unified sheet of parallel vertical threads.

**فَسَبِّحْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الْعَظِيمِ** — Q56:74 — "glorify the name of your Lord, **the Most Great**." The Qur'an commands glorification of العَظِيم. The PRACTICE of saying سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيم specifically in ruku is from the Sunnah (Hadith). The pose of maximum structural unity bears the name of العَظِيم.

## ROUND 8 — Hip and sacrum mechanics

**What was established:**

**Going INTO ruku:** The hip joint is the single node where everything pivots. The pelvis IS the bow (القَوْس / al-qaws). The posterior chain (hamstrings, glutes, sacrotuberous) IS the string (الوَتَر / al-watar). Going into ruku = drawing the bow. Energy stored.

**The sacrum:** The keystone (المَفْصِل العَجُزِي الحَرْقَفِي / al-Mafṣil al-ʿAjuzī al-Ḥarqafī). It NUTATES — rocks forward in its arch. In muqarnas terms, it's the squinch — the transition zone where horizontal force (spine in ruku) converts to vertical force (femurs). Most loaded point. Most commonly where things fail.

**Returning to qiyam:** The bow RELEASES. Sacrotuberous ligaments recoil. Elastic return, not muscular effort.

## ROUND 9 — The back warp is an inverted U (∩)

**Starting from عَجْب الذَّنَب / ʿajb al-dhanab / coccyx:**
- The Hadith: "All of the son of Adam will be consumed by the earth except the coccyx — from it he was created and from it he will be reassembled." (Sahih Muslim 2955)
- The coccyx IS the origin. The المِحْوَر الظَّهْرِي / al-miḥwar al-ẓahrī (stable conductor) goes straight from coccyx to عَقِب / ʿaqib / heel on each side.
- The back warp traces an ∩: heel → up → ischium → coccyx → ischium → down → heel
- The stable conductor and its nearest neighbours go STRAIGHT DOWN — they don't wrap the leg

**Ibn Sīnā (BA013):** "The STABLE CONDUCTOR — spine-aligned, occiput → sacrum → heel. All other lines reference this axis."

## ROUND 10 — Vertebra-by-vertebra branching

**Each vertebra from coccyx to waistline (L3) was mapped:**

- L5-L1: wrapping progressively further around the pelvis bowl via psoas

**L3-L4 specifically:** The psoas originates here, wraps FORWARD through the pelvis, and meets the stable conductor at the فَخِذ / fakhidh / femur. The psoas is the BRIDGE between lumbar spine and leg. Sitting shortens it → back pain.

## ROUND 11 — 11 warp lines per leg

**The spine divided into three sections:**
- UPPER (C1-C7): 7 vertebrae → shoulders/arms/neck
- MIDDLE (T1-T12): 12 vertebrae → torso wrap only (ribs)
- LOWER (Co + S5-S1 + L5-L1): **11 vertebrae → wrap to legs**

**Each of the 11 sends one warp per side, meeting the stable conductor at a different height along the leg.** Surface of leg ÷ 11 = even distribution. The coccyx warp meets at the top (ischium), the L1 warp meets near the ankle (having wrapped the furthest around).

## ROUND 12 — The textile metaphor is from Allah, not Ibn Sīnā

**bbi's correction:** The warp/weft textile metaphor is NOT Ibn Sīnā's — it's bbi's own analytical insight derived from **Q23:14**:

**فَكَسَوْنَا الْعِظَامَ لَحْمًا** — "Then We clothed (كَسَوْنَا / kasawnā) the bones with flesh (لَحْمًا / laḥman)"

Root ك-س-و / k-s-w = to clothe, to cover with a garment. Allah uses the SAME verb for:
- Clothing orphans with garments (Q4:5)
- Clothing bones with flesh (Q23:14)

The flesh IS a garment on the bone. The textile insight comes from **Allah's word choice** — He described flesh as clothing. If flesh is a garment, it has the properties of a garment.

**What Ibn Sīnā DID document (verified):** Force lines (خُطُوط / khuṭūṭ) — not threads. The stable conductor (BA013), the ventral lines (BA016-017), the lateral line (BA019), the spiral (BA020). bbi's textile insight **complements** Ibn Sīnā's force line architecture — the garment drapes over the force line skeleton.
